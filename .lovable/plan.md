

## First Question Analysis: Original Survey Intent → Spradley Door-Openers

Here's how each original Likert question maps to our approach, with 3 alternatives per theme ranked by fit:

### Contribution
**Original**: *"How often do you feel that you accomplish something and contribute?"*
**Intent**: Sense of impact, meaningful work, feeling useful

| # | Question | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **"What makes a good shift for you?"** | Invites concrete description of what accomplishment feels like to *them*. Easy to answer. AI probes for frequency/barriers naturally. |
| 2 | **"How does your work feel at the end of a shift?"** | Situational anchor (end of shift), invites emotional response. Very easy. |
| 3 | **"Do you feel like your work here matters?"** | Most direct. Easy yes/no entry, AI probes the "why" behind it. |

### Mindfulness
**Original**: *"How often do you feel nervous or stressed?"* + *"How often do you feel comfortable solving your problems and challenges?"*
**Intent**: Stress levels + problem-solving confidence (two sub-constructs)

| # | Question | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **"How's your stress level been at work lately?"** | Dead simple. Everyone understands it. One-word answer possible ("fine", "high"). AI can then probe for situations and coping. |
| 2 | **"When things get tricky at work, how does that feel?"** | Covers both stress AND problem-solving comfort in one question. Slightly more demanding to answer. |
| 3 | **"How do you handle it when things get busy?"** | Invites concrete coping description. Good for surfacing both stress and confidence. |

### Optimism
**Original**: *"How often do you feel optimistic about the future in Soundboks?"*
**Intent**: Future outlook, trust in direction, sense of trajectory

| # | Question | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **"How do you feel about where things are heading here?"** | Simple, open, directly maps to the construct. Easy one-line answer. |
| 2 | **"When you think about the next few months here, what comes up?"** | Concrete timeframe (important for part-timers who think shorter-term). |
| 3 | **"Do you see yourself here long-term?"** | Very direct. Might be too pointed for some, but powerful for part-timer context. |

### Working Experience (new — no original survey question)
**Intent**: Broad professional + social experience feedback from part-timers

| # | Question | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **"What's it like working here?"** | Maximally simple. 5 words. Invites both professional and social. |
| 2 | **"What stands out about working here?"** | Slightly more directed — pushes toward specifics immediately. |
| 3 | **"How would you describe working here to a friend?"** | Third-party framing lowers defensiveness, surfaces honest take. |

### Design principle at work

The original questions ask *"how often"* — frequency on a scale. We deliberately avoid that framing because:

- Frequency questions get scale answers ("sometimes", "often") — dead ends for conversation
- Our AI will naturally surface frequency through probing: *"Is that most shifts or just sometimes?"*, *"When was the last time that happened?"*
- Door-openers should invite a **feeling or situation**, not a measurement

The AI's 3-exchange-per-theme depth guarantee means each of these simple openers will be followed by 2 concrete probes — that's where the richness comes from.
